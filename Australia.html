Australia

Intro

Aboriginal Australians arrived on the continent at least 60,000 years ago and developed complex hunter-gatherer societies and oral histories.

Dutch navigators led by Abel Tasman were the first Europeans to land in Australia in 1606, and they mapped the western and northern coasts.

They named the continent New Holland but made no attempts to permanently settle it. In 1770, English captain James Cook sailed to the east coast of Australia, named it New South Wales, and claimed it for Great Britain.
